Sequence Alignment

What is Sequence Alignment?

Sequence alignment is a bioinformatics method used to compare DNA, RNA, or protein sequences to identify regions of similarity. These similarities help researchers understand evolutionary relationships, predict structure, and determine functional regions of biological sequences.

Types of Sequence Alignment

Pairwise Alignment

Compares two sequences to identify the best possible match between them.

Multiple Sequence Alignment

Aligns three or more sequences to detect conserved regions and evolutionary patterns.

Common Algorithms

  • Needleman–Wunsch — Used for global alignment.
  • Smith–Waterman — Performs local sequence alignment.
  • BLAST — Quickly finds similarity between biological sequences.
